Quality Assurance

Overview of Quality Assurance at WSU

The Quality Assurance (QA) function at Walter Sisulu University ensures that all academic and administrative activities meet institutional, national, and regulatory quality standards. Rooted in the Institutional Quality Management System Framework (IQMSF), the QA function provides structured mechanisms for evaluating performance, ensuring compliance, and driving continuous improvement across the university. It promotes accountability, strengthens academic integrity, and ensures that all programmes, services, and operations remain fit for purpose and aligned with the university's strategic direction.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Implementation of Quality Standards: Ensure institutional alignment with CHE standards, HEQSF requirements, professional body standards, and internal quality policies.
  • Quality Planning and Framework Management: Oversee the implementation and ongoing review of the IQMSF to ensure relevance, integration, and alignment with institutional goals.
  • Internal Quality Assurance Processes: Lead institutional QA processes across faculties, departments, and support units, including evaluations, audits, monitoring, and compliance checks.
  • Quality Review Coordination: Facilitate academic and administrative reviews, ensuring systematic processes for self-evaluation, evidence collection, peer review, and improvement planning.
  • Quality Assurance Systems Alignment: Support alignment between teaching and learning, research, community engagement, and administrative quality frameworks.
  • Policy Development and Assurance: Develop, review, and implement policies, guidelines, templates, and criteria that support consistent QA processes across the institution.
  • Assurance of Teaching and Learning Quality: Support curriculum quality assurance processes, module and programme assessments, and the enhancement of teaching practices.
  • Quality Evidence and Documentation: Manage evidence repositories that support compliance, review processes, and institutional reporting to regulatory bodies.
  • Risk-Focused Quality Assurance: Identify quality-related risks and support faculties and departments in implementing mitigating actions.
  • Support for External Evaluations: Prepare the institution for CHE evaluations, accreditation reviews, and professional body assessments.

The Quality Assurance function ensures that the university achieves consistent excellence across all domains by providing structured systems, rigorous evaluation processes, and institutional leadership in quality.

Quality Assurance Processes and Systems

Quality Assurance at WSU is grounded in the Plan–Do–Check–Act (PDCA) cycle, promoting reflection, accountability, and continuous improvement. QA processes evaluate performance and ensure that the university maintains a clear quality track record aligned with national expectations.

Core QA Processes:

  • Self-Evaluation Reports (SERs): Units conduct evidence-based evaluations of their performance, identifying strengths, gaps, and areas for improvement.
  • Peer Review Mechanisms: Internal and external reviewers provide objective assessments of institutional and programme quality.
  • Quality Improvement Plans (QIPs): Units develop actionable strategies to address review findings, monitored through continuous reporting.
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Track the implementation of QIPs, compliance with standards, and overall quality performance.
  • Review Cycles: Cyclical academic and support reviews ensure systematic evaluation and improvement over time.
  • Institutional Quality Reporting: Provide evidence-based reports to Senate, SQAC, EMC, and other governance structures.

These processes ensure that WSU maintains high levels of quality, transparency, and performance accountability in all academic and operational areas.

Quality Assurance Governance

Effective governance is essential for institutional quality. The QA function collaborates with leadership, faculties, departments, and committees to ensure that quality standards are upheld and that the university remains aligned with national and global higher education expectations.

Key Governance Roles:

  • Senate and SQAC: Provide strategic oversight, approve QA processes, and ensure compliance with academic quality standards.
  • Executive Management Committee (EMC): Review quality reports and support institutional decision-making informed by QA evidence.
  • Faculties and Departments: Implement QA systems, conduct SERs, develop QIPs, and maintain performance documentation.
  • Quality Practitioners and Quality Management Directorate: Coordinate QA activities, provide expertise, and support the professionalisation of quality practices.
  • External Bodies: Engage with CHE, SAQA, DHET, and professional councils to ensure accountability and accreditation success.

Through shared governance and collaboration, the Quality Assurance function ensures that the university operates within a strong quality ecosystem supported by clear roles, policies, and accountability mechanisms.
