Quality Enhancement and Monitoring

Institutional Quality Assurance & Continuous Improvement

The Quality Enhancement and Monitoring (QEM) function ensures that all academic and support departments at WSU maintain high standards of quality through systematic monitoring, evaluation, and reporting. This function works in alignment with the Institutional Quality Management System Framework (IQMSF) to promote accountability, compliance, and continuous improvement across the university.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Quality Monitoring and Evaluation: Conduct routine quality audits, reviews, and performance checks across academic and support departments.
  • Implementation of the IQMSF: Oversee the application of the Institutional Quality Management System Framework across departments and faculties.
  • Programme and Departmental Reviews: Coordinate cyclical reviews, self-evaluation reports (SERs), improvement plans, and follow-up evaluations.
  • Compliance Tracking: Ensure alignment with CHE standards, DHET requirements, and internal quality policies.
  • Risk Identification and Mitigation: Flag quality risks early and guide units on corrective action strategies.
  • Reporting and Feedback Processes: Produce institutional quality reports, review findings, dashboard insights, and performance summaries for Senate and Management.
  • Capacity Development for Quality: Train staff on quality assurance processes, reviews, self-evaluation, and action planning.
  • Support for Teaching, Learning, and Assessment Quality: Provide quality insights that strengthen curriculum delivery, assessment practices, and student learning experiences.
  • Monitoring of Follow-Up Actions: Track implementation of recommendations from audits, site visits, external evaluations, and review committees.

Quality Review and Institutional Accountability

This function strengthens institutional accountability by ensuring that quality review processes are executed thoroughly and consistently. It supports evidence-based decision-making and strengthens institutional governance.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Review Management: Coordinate internal and external quality reviews, including preparing units for CHE evaluations.
  • Standards and Criteria Alignment: Ensure that all reviews follow approved institutional and national standards.
  • Institutional Evidence Management: Manage quality portfolios, evidence repositories, and compliance documentation.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Work with faculties, support units, review panels, and external stakeholders to conduct effective reviews.
  • Quality Assurance Reporting: Produce comprehensive reports that inform institutional improvement decisions.
  • Quality Improvement Planning: Support units in designing actionable and measurable improvement plans post-review.

This function ensures that quality standards remain consistent across departments and that WSU continues to meet the expectations of internal and external quality authorities.

Performance Monitoring and Quality Data Insights

This function provides real-time data on quality performance indicators and institutional compliance. It ensures that decision-makers have accurate insights to strengthen academic quality.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Quality Dashboards: Develop dashboards that track review cycles, improvement plan implementation, compliance status, and quality indicators.
  • Monitoring of Quality KPIs: Analyse performance against teaching, learning, research, and support KPIs.
  • Risk Dashboarding: Identify emerging institutional quality risks and generate risk alerts.
  • Evidence-Based Reporting: Provide accurate data to support Senate, EMC, and quality governance structures.
  • Faculty and Departmental Support: Assist units in understanding their quality performance data.

This function transforms institutional quality data into actionable insights, supporting a culture of accountability, compliance, and continuous improvement at WSU.
