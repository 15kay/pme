Institutional Academic Activity Planning & Monitoring

Academic Activity Planning ensures that Walter Sisulu University operates within a structured, compliant, and strategically aligned academic framework. This function oversees the development of the academic calendar, monitors registrations, and ensures that all academic delivery processes follow regulatory and institutional standards.

The unit works closely with faculties, the Registrar’s Division, and other departments to ensure that admissions, registrations, and academic continuity processes are efficient, compliant, and aligned with institutional priorities. It ensures that academic service delivery is predictable, well-coordinated, and aligned with national expectations.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Academic Calendar Development: Lead the design and annual review of the institutional academic calendar.
  • Monitoring Admissions & Registrations: Track registrations and quotas to ensure proper alignment with the academic year and faculty capacities.
  • Academic Delivery Coordination: Oversee WSU’s academic service delivery model to ensure consistency and efficiency.
  • Educational Compliance: Monitor compliance with notional hour norms, academic integrity standards, and educational delivery requirements.
  • Faculty Advisory Support: Assist faculties with guidance on academic regulations, programme scheduling, and academic structure processes.
  • Operational Risk Monitoring: Identify registration or scheduling-related risks that could impact teaching and learning.

Through its planning and monitoring responsibilities, this function forms the backbone of academic operations at WSU. It ensures that the academic enterprise is stable, compliant, and positioned to deliver high-quality teaching and learning throughout the academic year.
