Coordination & Leadership

The Coordination & Leadership function ensures that academic planning activities across Walter Sisulu University are cohesive, collaborative, and aligned with institutional strategic priorities. This function strengthens communication, integrates planning efforts, and fosters a shared academic vision across faculties and administrative units.

Academic Planning plays a leadership role by facilitating workshops, engaging stakeholders, coordinating academic planning cycles, and supporting the development of innovative academic initiatives. The function ensures that all academic planning processes reflect collective ownership and institutional commitment.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Institutional Coordination: Align academic planning activities across faculties, directorates, and governance structures to ensure a unified institutional approach.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Facilitate collaborative planning sessions, workshops, and discussions to support new programme development, curriculum enhancement, and strategic projects.
  • Leadership in Academic Initiatives: Provide leadership in the conceptualisation and rollout of academic initiatives that advance institutional academic excellence.
  • Capacity Building: Support faculties and academic units through advisory services, training sessions, and resource-sharing to strengthen academic planning competencies.
  • Integrated Communication: Ensure clear and consistent communication across departments to support timely decision-making and coordinated academic operations.
  • Supporting Institutional Strategy: Align planning activities with the university’s strategic priorities and promote cohesion across academic and support units.

Through strong coordination and leadership, Academic Planning ensures that WSU’s academic enterprise is integrated, forward-thinking, and capable of delivering high-impact academic outcomes aligned with the institution’s mission and strategic direction.
