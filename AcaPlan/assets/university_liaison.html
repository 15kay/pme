University Liaison

The University Liaison function plays a crucial role in maintaining strong, collaborative relationships between Walter Sisulu University and key national higher education stakeholders. This function ensures that WSU remains aligned with regulatory, policy, and compliance expectations set by the Department of Higher Education and Training (DHET), the Council on Higher Education (CHE), and the South African Qualifications Authority (SAQA).

By actively participating in national forums, working groups, and policy dialogues, the Directorate ensures that WSU remains informed, responsive, and compliant with evolving higher education frameworks. It also coordinates institutional communication and submissions to ensure accuracy, coherence, and strategic alignment.

Roles and Responsibilities:

  • Stakeholder Engagement: Build and maintain relationships with DHET, CHE, SAQA, and other regulatory bodies.
  • Institutional Representation: Represent WSU in external committees, hearings, workshops, and policy formulation sessions.
  • Policy Coordination: Coordinate institutional responses to national policy changes, calls for comment, and reporting requirements.
  • Academic Programme Administration: Serve as the institutional administrator for programme submissions and external academic audits.
  • Regulatory Compliance Monitoring: Ensure adherence to national requirements for programme accreditation, registration, and ongoing quality assurance.
  • Internal Communication Facilitation: Share updates, notices, and interpretations of national policies with faculties and directorates.
  • Institutional Alignment Support: Help ensure that WSU’s academic activities and initiatives remain aligned with national higher education goals.

This function strengthens WSU’s visibility, credibility, and compliance within the national higher education system. By maintaining active, informed engagement with regulatory bodies, Academic Planning ensures that the university remains proactive and aligned with future academic and policy developments.
